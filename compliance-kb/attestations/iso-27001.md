# ISO/IEC 27001 (ISMS Certification)

Scope: a regime-specific attestation layer on top of [standards-kb](../../standards-kb/) Domains 04 (Security) & 09 (Compliance & Privacy). ISO/IEC 27001 certifies an **Information Security Management System (ISMS)** — a governed, risk-driven, continually-improving *management system*, not just a control checklist. Certification is issued by an accredited certification body after Stage 1 (documentation) and Stage 2 (implementation) audits, with annual surveillance and a 3-year recertification cycle.

> **Engineering guidance, not legal or audit advice.** This file helps engineers build and evidence the technical controls an ISMS depends on. ISMS scope, risk acceptance, the Statement of Applicability, and certification are owned by your security lead, top management, and the certification body. Nothing here substitutes for an accredited engagement.

## The ISMS (what actually gets certified)

The ISMS is the "clauses 4–10" management system. Auditors test that it *runs*, not just that it exists.

- **[MUST]** Define **context & scope** (clause 4): which products, teams, locations, and data the ISMS covers — narrow scope is legitimate but must be honest.
- **[MUST]** **Leadership & policy** (clause 5): top-management-approved information-security policy, roles, and responsibilities.
- **[MUST]** **Planning** (clause 6): risk assessment & treatment methodology, security objectives, and the **Statement of Applicability**.
- **[MUST]** **Support & operation** (clauses 7–8): resources, competence, awareness, documented information, and operational control of the treatment plan.
- **[MUST]** **Performance evaluation** (clause 9): monitoring, metrics, **internal audit**, and **management review**.
- **[MUST]** **Improvement** (clause 10): nonconformity handling, corrective actions, and continual improvement.

## Statement of Applicability (SoA)

The SoA is the central certification artifact: for **every** Annex A control it records *applicable / not applicable*, *justification*, and *implementation status*.

- **[MUST]** Produce an SoA covering all Annex A controls (2022 edition: **93 controls**), each with a decision and rationale, traceable to a risk.
- **[MUST]** Justify every **exclusion** — "not applicable" needs a defensible reason (e.g. "no in-house data centre → some physical controls handled by cloud provider under shared responsibility").
- **[SHOULD]** Link each applicable control to where it is implemented in standards-kb and to its evidence (see the mapping table).

## Risk assessment & treatment (the engine of the ISMS)

- **[MUST]** Maintain an **asset/risk inventory**; assess risks against confidentiality, integrity, availability with a repeatable methodology (impact × likelihood).
- **[MUST]** Choose a **treatment** per risk: *mitigate* (apply Annex A controls), *transfer* (insurance/vendor), *avoid*, or *accept* (with documented sign-off).
- **[MUST]** Produce a **Risk Treatment Plan** linking each unacceptable risk to the controls that reduce it and to an owner + due date.
- **[MUST]** Re-run risk assessment **at least annually** and on significant change.

## Evidence auditors ask for

- The **ISMS documentation set**: scope, security policy, risk methodology, **risk assessment + treatment plan**, and the **SoA**.
- **Records that the system runs**: internal-audit report, **management-review minutes**, corrective-action/nonconformity register, security objectives with metrics.
- **Operational evidence** for applicable Annex A controls (same artifacts as the mapping table), sampled across the period.
- Evidence of **continual improvement** — closed nonconformities, updated risks after incidents.

## Notes vs SOC 2

- **[SHOULD]** Reuse one control set for both: ISO adds the **management-system wrapper** (risk methodology, SoA, internal audit, management review); SOC 2 adds the **auditor's attestation report**. Continuous-compliance tooling can map a single control to both frameworks — see [soc2.md](soc2.md).

## Acceptance checklist

- [ ] ISMS scope, context, and top-management-approved security policy documented.
- [ ] Risk methodology defined; asset/risk inventory maintained; risks assessed (impact × likelihood).
- [ ] Risk Treatment Plan links each unacceptable risk to controls, owner, and due date.
- [ ] Statement of Applicability covers all 93 Annex A controls with justification + status; exclusions defensible.
- [ ] Applicable A.5/A.6/A.7/A.8 controls implemented in standards-kb and evidenced.
- [ ] Internal audit completed and management review held, with minutes.
- [ ] Nonconformity/corrective-action register in use; continual-improvement evidence exists.
- [ ] Backup restore-test and BCP/DR test records present.
- [ ] Supplier register, DPAs, and cloud shared-responsibility mapping maintained.
- [ ] Annual risk-assessment cadence and surveillance-audit readiness confirmed.
